Architecture and Monuments

The Mosque-Cathedral

A masterpiece of Islamic architecture, Cordoba's Mosque-Cathedral is famous for its two-tone red and white arches and forest of 856 columns. Built in the 8th century, it represents the apogee of Umayyad art in Spain.

Opening times: Mon-Sat: 10am–7pm, Sun: 8:30am–11:30am

Prices: Adults: 11€, Children (aged 10-14): 6€

The Alcázar of the Christian Monarchs

Royal fortress with magnificent terraced gardens, fountains, and pools. Its Arab gardens and defensive towers offer a glimpse into medieval military and palatial architecture.

Opening times: Tue–Sun: 8:30am–2:45pm

Prices: Adult: €8, Child: Free

Medina Azahara

Caliphal palace-city located 8km from Cordoba, built in the 10th century. This UNESCO archaeological site testifies to the splendor of Umayyad architecture and the power of the Caliphate of Cordoba.

Opening times: Tue–Sat: 9am–6pm, Sun: 9am–3pm

Prices: Entry: €5

The Roman Bridge

This monumental bridge from the 1st century BC spans the Guadalquivir River. Illuminated at night, it offers a spectacular view of the city and the Mosque-Cathedral.

Opening times: Open 24/7

Prices: Free

Corredera Square

Plaza de la Corredera is a unique rectangular square in Andalusia, located in the heart of Cordoba. Inspired by Castilian squares, it has hosted various historical events, including bullfights and public executions. Today, it is surrounded by cafés and bars, making it a popular spot for locals and visitors.

Opening times: Open 24/7

Prices: Free

The Palace of Viana

The Palacio de Viana, located in Cordoba, is a historic palace famous for its twelve beautifully designed patios and large garden. This palace-museum offers visitors a glimpse into Andalusian aristocratic architecture and the evolution of decorative styles over several centuries.

Opening times: Tue–Sun: 8:30am–2:45pm

Prices: Full visit: €12, Patios visit: €8, Children under 10: Free Free for patios visit on Wednesdays from 2pm to 3pm
