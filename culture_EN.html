Culture and Discovery

The Feria de Córdoba

The Feria de Córdoba is a popular festival held at the end of May in honor of the Virgin of Health. In El Arenal park, people dance the sevillana, attend bullfights, eat tapas, and enjoy the casetas and rides late into the night.

Hours: Last week of May: 24/7

Admission: Free access

The Patios of Córdoba

A tradition inscribed on UNESCO's Intangible Cultural Heritage list, the flowered patios are the soul of Córdoba. In May, the patio festival allows visitors to discover these jewels of domestic architecture.

Hours: Festival in May, some patios open year-round

Admission: Varies depending on patio

Museo de Bellas Artes de Córdoba

The Museo de Bellas Artes de Córdoba is dedicated to painting and sculpture, highlighting works by Spanish artists from the Middle Ages to the present. Located in the former Hospital de la Caridad, it features rich collections of baroque paintings and Andalusian sculptures.

Hours: Tues-Sat: 9am-9pm, Sun and holidays: 9am-3pm (Closed Monday)

Admission: Free for Europeans, €1.50 otherwise

The Feria Entrance Arch

The "Portada" of the Feria de Córdoba is a monumental structure marking the entrance to the El Arenal fairgrounds. It is one of the most iconic symbols of this festival, held each year in late May in honor of Our Lady of Health.​

Tablao Flamenco El Cardenal

Located in Córdoba's historic center, the Tablao Flamenco El Cardenal is a renowned venue with over 30 years of history, offering authentic flamenco shows featuring award-winning artists.​

Hours: Between 8pm and 9:30pm

Admission: Adults: €25, Children: €12

Teatro de la Axerquía

The Teatro de la Axerquía is an open-air amphitheater located in Córdoba, Spain. Inaugurated in 1975, it was designed by architect José Rebollo and is integrated into Cruz Conde park. With a capacity of over 3,500 spectators, it hosts a variety of events, including concerts, plays, and festivals.

Hours: Depends on the events

Admission: Varies by event

Roman Temple of Córdoba

Located near the current city hall, the Roman Temple of Córdoba is the only confirmed Roman temple in the city, its existence proven through archaeological evidence. Dedicated to imperial worship, it impresses with its large size and was part of the Provincial Forum, alongside a circus. Built in marble, it originally featured six Corinthian columns on the façade and stood on a podium accessed by stairs.​

Zoo Conservation Center of Córdoba

The Córdoba Zoo Conservation Center is a zoo focused on conservation and environmental education, located in Córdoba, Spain. It is home to a variety of animal species and offers educational programs to raise awareness about biodiversity and wildlife protection.

Hours: 9:30am to 7:00pm​ (Closed in August)

Admission: Free for Europeans, €1.50 otherwise
