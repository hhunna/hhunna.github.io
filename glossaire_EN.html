Glossary

Baroque Art:

An artistic style that emerged in Europe in the 17th century, characterized by its richness, exaggerated forms, movement, and spectacular effects. It aims to impress and move, whether in painting, sculpture, architecture, or music.
more...

Caliphate:

A political and religious system in which the leader, called the caliph, is regarded as the successor to the Prophet Muhammad. He exercises both spiritual authority over Muslims and political power over a territory.
more...

Corinth :

Corinth is a port and trading city in the Peloponnese, Greece, capital of the regional district of Corinthia and of the Corinthian demesne, with a population of 56,437 in 2021. It is located on the Isthmus of Corinth, in the gulf of the same name.
more...

Corrida (Bullfight):

A traditional spectacle, especially in Spain and some parts of Latin America, where a matador faces a bull in an arena. The corrida is both a cultural ritual and a subject of controversy, particularly due to the animal suffering it involves.
more...

Flamenco:

An art form originating from Andalusia that combines singing (cante), dancing (baile), and guitar playing (toque). Flamenco is known for its intensity, passionate rhythms, and deep emotional expression.
more...

Umayyad:

The Umayyads (in Arabic: الأمويون al-ʾUmawiyyūn, or بنو أمية Banū ʾUmayyah) were an Arab dynasty that ruled the Muslim world from 661 to 750 and then Al-Andalus from 756 to 1031. They are named after their ancestor ʾUmayyah ibn ʿAbd Shams, the great-uncle of the Prophet Muhammad.
more...

Patios :

A patio (Spanish word from Latin pateo) is an open-air interior living space, typically square in shape and located at the center of a house—common in traditional Mediterranean vernacular architecture. It is usually surrounded by a covered walkway. Its private use distinguishes it from a courtyard, which is a semi-public passage area between the street and the house. In the U.S. and Canada (Acadia, Quebec), “patio” may refer to a simple, uncovered terrace.
more...

Tablao :

A place or stage dedicated to flamenco performances, often found in a bar or small theater. It is where artists (singers, dancers, guitarists) perform for an audience in an intimate and authentic setting.
